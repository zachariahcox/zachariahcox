## Business justification (400 words or less)

Why does the team need a new role? Write a job description.  
What is the team?  
Is there enough charter for this new role to be successful for at least the next 2 years?  
Why now?

## Why this candidate fits the role (400 words or less)

Similar to the "summary" we use during hiring.   
What skills and abilities are brought?  
How do they improve the team culture?

## Accomplishments (400 words or less)

Subject matter expertise  
Links to artifacts of completed projects  
Should demonstrate exceeding expectations of current level.  
Non-technical accomplishments (mentorships, speaking engagements, etc).

## Endorsements 

Choose 3 to 5 peers to provide feedback.   
Each peer should be at-or-above the target audience.   
For Staff level and above, at least one peer must come from outside the candidates team.  
Endorsements should be sorted from *most* to *least* useful to the reader (not by level or reputation of the endorser).

## Question template for peers

The audience for these questions are leaders who may or may not be familiar with the candidate.  
Please use examples and observations where possible and avoid generalizations ("the candidate is the best engineer of all time.")

### What is your name, role and relation to the candidate? 

`Zac, Senior Engineer. I have worked with the candidate for 2 years.`

### Do you recommend promoting this candidate to \<Insert proposed level here\> (yes / no)?

`Yes / no.`

### Why is this candidate a good fit for the new role? (400 words or less)

It's often helpful to reference career ladders.
During project `foo`, they demonstrated `bar`.

### What is an area where this candidate should focus to improve their impact?

This is a calibrating question, helping to quantify how well the endorser actually knows the candidate.
